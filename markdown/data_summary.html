Numbers of viruses, sequences

  • 1585 viruses in family Coronaviridae identified through NCBI taxonomy.
  • Of these 1585, 573 (36.2%) have at least one available spike protein nucleotide sequence.
  • Of these 573, 53 (9.2%) have at least one recognised mammal or bird host in EID2 (which may be human host) NB: virtually all coronaviruses are viruses of birds/mammals
  • Total sequence accessions: 16147, made up of 40801 individual protein coding sequences

SARS-CoV-2 sequences

  • 151 spike protein nucleotide sequences identified by programmatic extraction from GenBank
  • However, manual extraction from GenBank (download from SARS-CoV-2 landing page at 2020-03-27 18:15:04 GMT) find additional sequences not contained in these searches because metadata incorrectly labels them under taxid 694009 for SARS-CoV (the parent species).
  • 95 sequences identified from manual extraction from GenBank (not as many as above, even with additional sequences, because again, not fully annotated, so can’t extract from searches)
  • 2239 sequences identified from manual extraction from GISAID (all complete genomes, though proteins are not annotated..)
